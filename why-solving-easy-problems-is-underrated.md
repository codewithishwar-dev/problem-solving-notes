# Why Solving Easy Problems is Underrated

Many developers jump straight to **medium or hard coding problems** when practicing on platforms like LeetCode.

It feels more challenging.  
It feels more impressive.

But experienced developers often take a different approach.

They spend time mastering the **fundamentals through easy problems**.

---

## Why Easy Problems Matter

Easy coding problems introduce the **core patterns** used in more complex problems.

Problems such as:

- Two Sum
- Valid Parentheses
- Longest Common Prefix

may look simple, but they teach important concepts like:

- **Hash Maps** – fast lookups
- **Stacks** – handling ordered operations
- **String Traversal** – iterating through characters efficiently

These patterns appear again and again in more advanced problems.

---

## Pattern Recognition

Once you recognize these patterns, solving medium problems becomes much easier.

| Problem | Pattern | Concept |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Two Sum | Hash Map | Fast lookup |
| Valid Parentheses | Stack | Balanced structure |
| Longest Common Prefix | String Traversal | Character comparison |

Learning these patterns through easy problems helps build a **strong foundation**.

---

## Common Mistake

Many developers skip easy problems because they believe they are too simple.

But skipping easy problems often means **skipping the fundamentals**.

Strong developers focus on understanding **how problems work**, not just solving difficult ones.

---

## Key Takeaway

Great developers don’t rush difficulty.

They build **strong fundamentals first**, and easy problems are one of the best ways to develop problem-solving intuition.
